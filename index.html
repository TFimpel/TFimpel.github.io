Automating Godatabase Attachment Uploads
Adding geodatabase attachments via ArcMap is time consming, and keeping hundreds of folder directories in sync with geodatabase attachments is tedious at best. This python script forms the backbone of an ArcMap geoprocessing tool that (1) traverses a standardized folder directory, (2) identifies files that are to be added as geodatabase attachments, and (3) adds the files to the appropriate feature within the appropriate feature class - parameters that are derived from each file's directory path. You can download the .py script here.

Spatial Databases and 3D Geometries

Class project: 3D Geometries, SQL Trigger Functions, and a 2.5D Interior Asset Management GIS
Is 3D worth it? The potential use cases for GIS are far too diverse for a generic answer. But given the 3D spatial types and functions that common spatial database systems offer the question does need to be asked and answered, albeit on a case by case basis.

This fun project consisted of several parts centered around three dimensional spatial data. The write up, which you can download here, loosely follows the steps I carried out and is broken into two main sections. The first section describes my explorations of the 3D capabilities that postgres/postgis databases offer. The second section describes my explorations of the 3D capabilities that ESRI ArcSDE geodatabases and ArcScene's on-the-fly feature extrusion and layer floating tools offer. A common theme in both sections is the use of spatial data for interior asset management purposes.

At the end of the second section I lay out a practical use case discussing why and how 2.5D spatial data may the best fit for an interior asset management GIS using example data from the University of Minnesota Enterprise GIS database.

Urban GIS

Urban areas are shaped by spatial processes that are incredibly complex. Many organizations, businesses, and governemnt agencies utilize GIS to understand these processes and carry out a variety of adminstrative functions.

Affordable Housing and Neighborhod Resilience
In this class project me and my team members used GIS to measure possible impacts of an affordable housing development in South Minneapolis on neighborhood housing sales.

Past and present US Census data as well as property sales and home improvemnt permit records were incorporated to capture any potential patterns. The processing and analysis was primarily carried out with ArcGIS Desktop software.

Remote Sensing

Many GIS information products rely to some degree on remotely sensed data. A basic undertanding of common remote sensing technologies and their capabilities and limitations sould be part of every GIS professional's toolkit.

Landcover Change Detection
The goal of this course project was to identify and compare land cover changes in the 8 primary watersheds of the Twin Cities metro area between 1984 - 2009.

Past and present Landsat imagery was used in combination with watershed boundary geometries from the MNDNR. The processing and analysis was primarily carried out with ERDAS Imagine software.

Landsat Raster Data, USGS

Of particular intrest was land cover change due to urban growth. Watershed health is heavily impacted when previously agricultural or undeveloped lands are turned into subdivisions, shopping malls, and industrial parks.
